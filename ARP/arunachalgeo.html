Geographical Features

Arunachal Pradesh is a land of breathtaking landscapes, featuring towering Himalayan peaks, dense forests, and numerous rivers. The state is known for its diverse ecosystem, which includes tropical, subtropical, and alpine zones.

The state's geographical features contribute to its rich biodiversity, hosting rare flora and fauna found nowhere else in the world.

Glacial lakes and waterfalls dot the landscape, making Arunachal Pradesh one of the most visually stunning states in India.

The Brahmaputra River originates from Tibet and flows through the state as the Siang River, providing vital water resources.

The terrain varies from steep mountains in the north to lush valleys and plateaus in the south, creating a mix of microclimates.

Numerous valleys such as Ziro, Tawang, and Mechuka are nestled among the mountains, offering breathtaking views and rich cultural heritage.

The state also features alpine meadows and subtropical forests, creating diverse vegetation and wildlife habitats.

Several glaciers in the upper Himalayan region contribute to the river systems, sustaining agriculture and daily life in the valleys.

Natural hot springs are scattered across the state, attracting visitors for their reputed healing properties.

The unique river islands, marshlands, and floodplains contribute to the state's ecological balance and hydrological cycle.

Key Geographical Highlights

  • The state is home to the Eastern Himalayas, with peaks reaching over 7,000 meters.
  • Major rivers like the Siang, Subansiri, Kameng, and Dibang flow through the region.
  • The state features deep gorges and high mountain passes, crucial for trade routes.
  • Arunachal Pradesh has one of India’s largest forest covers, rich in biodiversity.
  • The region experiences diverse climatic zones, from tropical rainforests to snowy peaks.
  • The Sela Pass, at 4,170 meters, is one of the most beautiful high-altitude passes in India.
  • Many caves and limestone formations exist, adding to the state’s natural beauty.
  • Subtropical forests dominate the lower elevations, transitioning into alpine meadows higher up.
  • The state is prone to landslides and seismic activity due to its tectonic location.
  • It has abundant mineral resources, including coal, oil, and limestone.
