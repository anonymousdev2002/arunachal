Arunachal Pradesh Economy & Industries
Driving Growth Through Sustainable Development

Key Economic Sectors

Arunachal Pradesh, located in the northeastern region of India, has a diverse economy driven by agriculture, hydropower, tourism, and forestry. The state is rich in natural resources, offering immense potential for industrial growth and economic development.

The agriculture sector remains the backbone of the state's economy, with a majority of the population engaged in farming. Major crops include rice, maize, millet, and horticultural produce like oranges, apples, and kiwis.

Hydropower is a significant contributor to Arunachal Pradesh's economy, with the state possessing vast water resources ideal for generating renewable energy. Several large-scale hydropower projects have been initiated to harness this potential.

Tourism plays a vital role in economic development, attracting visitors with its breathtaking landscapes, monasteries, and adventure opportunities. The sector generates employment and contributes to the local economy.

The forest industry is another major contributor, with a rich reserve of timber and non-timber forest products. Sustainable forestry practices are being encouraged to balance economic benefits with environmental conservation.

Major Industries

  • Agriculture & Horticulture - Major cash crops include rice, maize, oranges, and apples.
  • Hydropower - Arunachal Pradesh has one of the highest hydroelectric potentials in India.
  • Tourism - Eco-tourism, adventure tourism, and cultural tourism drive economic activity.
  • Forestry & Timber - Sustainable forest management supports the economy and employment.
  • Handloom & Handicrafts - Indigenous communities produce traditional textiles and crafts.
  • Mining - Rich in minerals like coal, dolomite, and graphite, offering industrial opportunities.
  • Tea Plantations - The state is emerging as a tea-producing hub, with high-quality organic tea.
  • Fisheries - Promoting fish farming and aquaculture to boost rural incomes.
  • Food Processing - Encouraging agro-based industries to add value to local produce.
  • Sericulture - Production of high-quality silk, particularly eri and muga silk.
  • Textile Industry - Traditional weaves and modern fabric production contribute to the economy.
  • Handicraft Cooperatives - Supporting artisans to preserve and promote traditional crafts.
  • Livestock & Dairy - Expanding dairy farming and poultry production for economic growth.
  • Infrastructure Development - Investments in roads, bridges, and urbanization drive progress.
  • Renewable Energy - Apart from hydropower, solar and wind energy projects are being developed.
  • IT & Digital Economy - Government initiatives are promoting digital transformation and startups.
  • Export-Oriented Industries - Promoting trade of agricultural and handicraft products.
  • Organic Farming - Encouraging sustainable and chemical-free farming practices.
  • Biotechnology - Exploring medicinal plant-based industries for economic growth.
  • Transport & Logistics - Enhancing connectivity to facilitate trade and industrial expansion.
